AZOGUE is a Mexican Gothic horror project in `/home/david/Mexico Horror/`, written in
Mexican Spanish, in the tradition of Guillermo del Toro. Setting: Real de Plata, Zacatecas,
1975, during the Guerra Sucia. A 10-year-old, Ximena Salcido, descends into a flooded mine
(Pozo del Ahorcado) where the army dumps disappeared miners, and trades her own true
memories to a mercury creature (El Sinnombre) for the names of the dead.

**Why:** David asked to serialize the existing feature treatment into a 10-episode Netflix
limited series. That work is done (2026-07-02).

**How to apply:** Any future AZOGUE writing must not contradict established canon. Source of
truth files, in order of authority:
- `Azogue guion.md` — original creative bible + opening sequence (scenes 1–7). Highest canon.
- `Azogue - Biblia de Serie.md` — my series bible. Contains the INVIOLABLE trade rules
  (§3, Regla 1–7), the per-episode cost ladder (§5), all talisman final states (§8).
- `Azogue - Outlines de Episodios.md` — 10 episode treatments.
- `Azogue - Episodio 01 (guion completo).md` — full teleplay; scenes 1–7 are verbatim canon,
  8–22 are new.
- `guillermo-del-toro.skill` (zip w/ SKILL.md) — the writing methodology. Not in the loaded
  Skill list; read it directly if needed.

**Load-bearing rules that are easy to break:** (1) The payment is always a *narrated true
memory*, and she loses exactly what she narrates — never an object or bare word. The Ep10
finale trade is NOT "hand over her name as a token" (that would break the mechanic); it is
narrating **the root-memory of learning that "Ximena" meant her**, and losing it. That root
memory is the heaviest (everything hangs off it), which is why one trade releases ALL names at
once (Regla 4). (2) El Sinnombre can't speak/take first and only takes what's freely given;
more honest than any human. (3) Guzmán is a true believer, never a cartoon; his ruin is
private (DFS cuts him loose) and un-redeemed — he ends haunted, NOT contrite. (4) Ending is
costly: names return, the disappeared men do NOT; the system survives. (5) Fichas carry
NUMBERS not names — so sealing the pozo (entombing the creature) really does threaten the
names; that's the finale clock. (6) Ximena keeps ONE deliberate "spark" of self in Ep9 so the
near-emptied child can still consciously *choose* the Ep10 sacrifice (agency fix). (7)
Company wants to REOPEN the pozo, DFS wants to SEAL it — that conflict is the back-half delay
engine. (8) Carbide lamp = master talisman; passes to Teresa in finale. (9) The pozo is LOCAL
geography — everything it holds was thrown into THIS pozo; not cosmic/all-mercury. (10)
Mictlán/Tezcatlipoca is Doña Cleofas's diegetic belief, NOT a structural episode cage. These
were pinned during a finale pressure-test (2026-07-02). See [[user-david-creative]].
